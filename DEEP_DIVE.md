# Steel Dynamics Deep Dive

## Snapshot
- Headquarters: Fort Wayne, Indiana. [^1]
- Public company (NYSE: STLD).
- Scale: estimated steelmaking and steel coating capacity about 16 million tons; one of the largest domestic steel producers and metal recyclers in the US; diversified product and end-market portfolio with downstream fabrication; investing in aluminum flat-rolled products with high recycled content for beverage can, automotive, and industrial markets. [^1]
- Primary revenue sources: steel products, recycled ferrous and nonferrous metals, and steel joist/deck fabrication. [^1]

## Business model and segments
### Steel Operations
- EAF steel mills using ferrous scrap and scrap substitutes; continuous casting and automated rolling; coating, processing, and warehousing. [^1]
- Sells to end users, processors, fabricators, and service centers across construction, automotive, manufacturing, transportation, heavy equipment, energy, and pipe/tube markets. [^1]
- Net sales mix: 69% (2024), 67% (2023), 65% (2022). Export share: 6% (2024), 8% (2023), 5% (2022). [^1]

### Steel Fabrication Operations
- Seven New Millennium Building Systems plants serving non-residential construction nationwide; products include joists, girders, and steel deck. [^1]
- Net sales mix: 10% (2024), 15% (2023), 19% (2022). Volume: 607k tons (2024), 663k (2023), 856k (2022). [^1]

### Metals Recycling Operations
- Ferrous and nonferrous scrap processing, transportation, brokerage, and scrap management, located near SDI mills and other end users across the US and Mexico. [^1]
- Net sales mix: 11% (2024/2023), 9% (2022). Export share: 17% (2024), 19% (2023), 14% (2022). [^1]
- 2024 shipments: 5.85M gross tons ferrous; 62% shipped to SDI mills; 965M pounds nonferrous. [^1]

### Aluminum Operations (growth platform)
- Investing in aluminum flat-rolled products with high recycled content, targeting beverage can, automotive, and industrial markets. [^1]
- Properties include Aluminum Dynamics (Columbus, MS) flat-rolled mill, Aluminum Dynamics of Mexico slab facility, and Superior Aluminum Alloys in New Haven, IN. [^1]

## Differentiation and strategy
- Six strategic pillars: Health and Safety, Entrepreneurial Culture, Customer Commitment, Strategic Sustainable Growth, Innovation, and Financial Strength. [^1]
- Over 70% of steel and steel fabrication sales are value-added; focus on supply-chain solutions that improve efficiency and support decarbonization. [^1]
- Vertical integration connects metals recycling with EAF steelmaking and downstream fabrication, supporting cost and supply flexibility. [^1]

## Sustainability and decarbonization
- Scrap-based EAF model: recycled ferrous scrap typically makes up more than 80% of the metallic melt mix for EAF steelmaking. [^1]
- Science-based GHG emissions intensity targets certified by the Global Steel Climate Council; targets include a 2050 intensity target and an interim 2030 target for steel mills. [^2]
- Joint venture with Aymium (SDI Biocarbon Solutions) to build a biocarbon production facility supplying renewable carbon to EAF mills. [^2]
- Company position emphasizes science-based GHG targets and certified products. [^3]

## Market dynamics and risks (from 10-K)
- Cyclical steel industry with exposure to construction, automotive, and manufacturing demand shifts. [^1]
- Scrap and energy price volatility can pressure margins; competition from domestic and foreign producers and substitute materials. [^1]
